The 2025 season was the 18th season for the Indian Premier League (IPL) cricket franchise Royal Challengers Bengaluru. They were one of the ten teams that competed in the 2025 Indian Premier League. Ahead of the season, Rajat Patidar was announced as the captain. The team was coached by Andy Flower. The Royal Challengers Bengaluru finished in second place in the league stage, and advanced to the Qualifier 1 in playoffs. They also became the first IPL team to win all away games in a season. The team defeated Punjab Kings in the Qualifier 1 to qualify for Bengaluru's fourth final. In the final, they defeated Punjab Kings again to win their maiden title after 18 years. Bengaluru's Virat Kohli scored the most runs (657) while, Josh Hazlewood took the most wickets (22) for Bengaluru in the 2025 season. Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) retained three players ahead of the IPL 2025 Auction: Virat Kohli (₹21 crore), Rajat Patidar (₹11 crore), and Yash Dayal (₹5 crore), spending a total of ₹37 crore on retention. The team spent ₹82.25 crore in the auction, leaving ₹75 lakh in their purse. RCB acquired 19 new players, bringing their total squad to 22 players (14 Indian, 8 overseas).
