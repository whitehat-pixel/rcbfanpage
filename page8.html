RCB 2025 PLAYOFF JURNY

Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) ended their 18-year wait for an IPL title by winning the 2025 Indian Premier League, defeating Punjab Kings (PBKS) by 6 runs in a thrilling final at the Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad. This victory marked RCB’s first IPL championship, breaking a long streak of final appearances without a win (2009, 2011, 2016). Key Highlights of RCB’s 2025 Season: Final Result: Champions (Defeated PBKS in Final). Season Record: 9 wins, 4 losses, 1 no result (19 points, 2nd in points table). Captain: Rajat Patidar (led the team to its maiden title). Coach: Andy Flower. Home Ground: M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bengaluru. Unprecedented Away Performance: RCB made history by becoming the first team in IPL history to win all seven of their away matches in a single season. This included a significant win over Chennai Super Kings at Chepauk, ending a 16-year winless streak at that venue. Virat Kohli (RCB): Scored 43 off 35 balls, anchoring the innings and playing a crucial role in building momentum after early setbacks. Liam Livingstone (RCB): Contributed 25 off 15 balls, providing vital acceleration in the middle overs. Jitesh Sharma (RCB): Hit 24 off 10 balls, including two sixes, to boost the scoring rate late in the innings. Romario Shepherd (RCB): Smashed 17 off 9 balls in the final overs, though he was dismissed in the 19th over. Krunal Pandya (RCB): Took 2 wickets for 17 runs in 4 overs, earning Player of the Match honors for his match-defining spell with the ball. Shashank Singh (PBKS): Scored a match-winning 61 off 30 balls, including 6 sixes, but fell short as PBKS were bowled out for 184/7. Match Summary: RCB posted 190/9 in their 20 overs, with Arshdeep Singh (PBKS) taking 3 wickets in the final over. PBKS chased 191, reaching 184/7, falling 6 runs short. Bhuvneshwar Kumar (RCB) took 2 wickets in the 16th over, including the key wicket of Marcus Stoinis, sealing the game. Final Note: While no single player ran throughout the entire match, the collective effort of RCB’s squad—especially Kohli, Livingstone, and Krunal Pandya—led to the franchise’s first IPL title. The victory was celebrated as a historic milestone, with Kohli shedding tears of joy after lifting the trophy.
