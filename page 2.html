RCB HISTORY – 2008

Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) was founded in 2008 when the Indian Premier League (IPL) was officially launched by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI). The idea of a city-based T20 league was announced in September 2007 after India’s T20 World Cup win. Bengaluru was selected as one of the eight original teams.

Businessman Vijay Mallya, then chairman of United Spirits, bought the Bengaluru franchise for $111.6 million, the second-highest bid in the first IPL auction. He named the team “Royal Challengers Bangalore,” inspired by the Royal Challenge brand. The team’s colours—red, gold, and black—were chosen to reflect power and bold identity.

RCB played their first-ever IPL match on April 18, 2008, against Kolkata Knight Riders in the opening match of the tournament. Despite having legends like Rahul Dravid, Anil Kumble, Jacques Kallis, and Wasim Jaffer, the team struggled to adapt to the fast-paced T20 format and finished 7th that season.

However, 2008 became an important foundation year. Fans quickly connected with the team’s energy and identity. The M. Chinnaswamy Stadium became known for electrifying cricket, loud chants, and big-hitting matches.

The lessons learned from 2008 pushed the franchise to build a stronger T20-focused lineup in the coming years. This led to signing big talents like Chris Gayle, AB de Villiers, Kevin Pietersen, and strengthening Virat Kohli’s long-term role. The 2008 season laid the base for RCB to become one of the IPL’s most loved teams.
