RCB HISTORY OF 2016

Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) had a standout 2016 Indian Premier League (IPL) season, finishing as runners-up after a strong campaign.

Captain: Virat Kohli led the team, delivering a record-breaking performance by scoring 973 runs—the highest in IPL history at the time—earning him the Orange Cap and the Most Valuable Player award.

Season Performance: RCB finished second in the league stage with 8 wins and 6 losses, accumulating 16 points and a net run rate (NRR) of +0.932. They secured a spot in the playoffs after a strong mid-season resurgence, including a record 144-run victory over Gujarat Lions—the largest margin of victory in IPL history.

Playoffs Journey:
Qualifier 1: Defeated Gujarat Lions by 4 wickets to advance to their third IPL final.
Final: Lost to Sunrisers Hyderabad by 8 runs at the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bangalore, ending their quest for their first IPL title.

Team Records: Virat Kohli scored 4 centuries in the season. Shane Watson and Yuzvendra Chahal were the second and third highest wicket-takers in the tournament with 20 wickets each. The 229-run partnership between Kohli and de Villiers remains one of the most memorable batting displays in IPL history.

The 2016 season marked RCB’s most successful campaign to date, highlighted by individual brilliance and a deep team performance, though they fell short of winning their maiden IPL trophy. .

Virat Kohli (born 5 November 1988 in New Delhi, India) is an Indian international cricketer and former all-format captain of the Indian national team. He is a right-handed top-order batter and occasional right-arm medium pace bowler, widely regarded as one of the greatest all-format batsmen in cricket history. Key Career Highlights: International Career: Made his ODI debut on 18 August 2008 against Sri Lanka, Test debut on 20 June 2011 against West Indies, and T20I debut on 12 June 2010 against Zimbabwe. Captaincy: Former captain across all three formats—Test, ODI, and T20I. He is the most successful Test captain in Indian cricket history, with 40 wins in 68 matches and 3 consecutive Test mace retainments. Records & Achievements: Most ODI centuries (50+ as of 2023, a world record). Second-most international centuries (85 across all formats). First player to score 20,000 international runs in a single decade (2010–2019). ICC Cricketer of the Decade (2011–2020). Only batter to earn 900+ rating points across all three formats. First Indian to hold the number one spot in ICC rankings for Test, ODI, and T20I batting simultaneously (2018). T20I & IPL: Scored his first T20I century in 2022. Leading run-scorer in IPL history with over 8,000 runs for Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB), who he captained from 2013 to 2021. Won the IPL title with RCB in 2025. Major Team Successes: 2008 U19 World Cup (captain). 2011 ODI World Cup (key contributor). 2013 Champions Trophy. 2024 T20 World Cup. 2025 Champions Trophy. Personal Life: Married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma since December 2017. Children: Daughter Vamika (born 11 January 2021), son Akaay (born 15 February 2024). Net Worth: Estimated at INR 1,000 Crore. Social Media: Over 266 million followers on Instagram. Philanthropy: Founder of the Virat Kohli Foundation, supporting young athletes and promoting sports in India. Recent Developments: Retired from Test cricket after the 3 January 2025 vs Australia match. Last ODI: 18 January 2026 vs New Zealand. Last T20I: 29 June 2024 vs South Africa. Currently plays for Delhi in domestic cricket and Royal Challengers Bengaluru in the IPL
