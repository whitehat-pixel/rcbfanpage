VIRAT KHOLI

Virat Kohli is an Indian international cricketer, born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi, India. He is a right-handed top-order batter and has played for India across all formats since his debut in 2008. Kohli captained India in Test, ODI, and T20I cricket, becoming the most successful Test captain in Indian history with the most wins and three consecutive Test mace retainments. He holds the record for the most centuries in ODI cricket and is second in international cricket with 85 centuries across formats. Kohli is also the leading run-scorer in the Indian Premier League (IPL) and the first player to score 20,000 international runs in a single decade. He was ranked number one in all three ICC batting formats at different times, making him the only Indian to achieve this. Kohli has won ten ICC Awards, the most by any player, including Cricketer of the Year (2017, 2018) and Leading Cricketer in the World (2017–2019). He was named Cricketer of the Decade (2011–2020) by the ICC and received India’s highest sporting honor, the Khel Ratna Award (2018), and the Padma Shri (2017). He played a key role in India’s 2011 ODI World Cup, 2013 Champions Trophy, 2024 T20 World Cup, and 2025 Champions Trophy victories. Kohli announced his retirement from Test cricket on 12 May 2025 and from T20Is after the 2024 T20 World Cup final. On a personal front, he is married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma since 2017. They have two children: Vamika (born 2021) and Akaay (born 2024). He is also the founder of the Virat Kohli Foundation, supporting underprivileged children through sports.

RAJAT PATIDAR

Rajat Manohar Patidar is an Indian cricketer born on 1 June 1993 in Indore, Madhya Pradesh. He is a right-handed top-order batter and a right-arm off-break bowler, known for his aggressive stroke play and consistency in domestic and franchise cricket. Early Life and Career Patidar began playing cricket at the age of 8 and was initially a bowler before focusing on batting after his U-15 level. He made his first-class debut for Madhya Pradesh on 30 October 2015 in the Ranji Trophy and his T20 debut in January 2018. He emerged as the leading run-scorer for Madhya Pradesh in the 2018–19 Ranji Trophy, scoring 713 runs in eight matches. Franchise Career (IPL) IPL Debut: April 2021 with Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB). 2021: Scored 71 runs in 4 matches and was released. 2022: Signed as a mid-season replacement; scored a record-breaking 112 not out off 49 balls in the Eliminator against Lucknow Super Giants — the first uncapped player to score a century in an IPL playoff match. 2023: Retained by RCB but missed the season due to an Achilles heel injury. 2024: Scored 395 runs in 13 innings at a strike rate of 177.13, including 5 half-centuries. 2025: Appointed captain of RCB after Faf du Plessis’ departure and Virat Kohli’s decision to focus solely on batting. Under his leadership, RCB won their first-ever IPL title. International Career ODI Debut: 21 December 2023 against South Africa, scoring 22 runs in 16 balls. Test Debut: 2 February 2024 against England at Visakhapatnam, scoring 32 and 9 in the two innings. Role: Primarily a middle-order batter; has been named in India A and domestic squads for red-ball series. Domestic and Leadership Roles Captain of Madhya Pradesh in the Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy 2024–25, where he scored 428 runs in 9 innings at an average of 61.14, including a 81* against Mumbai. Holds the record for the 5th fastest century in Ranji Trophy history (100 off 68 balls vs Haryana). Personal Life Height: Approximately 5 ft 7 in (170 cm). Jersey Number: 87 (India), 19 (IPL), 87 (Domestic). Married to Gunjan Patidar since 7 July 2022. Hobbies: Travelling, golf, cycling. Influences: Virat Kohli and Cheteshwar Pujara. Achievements Summary First uncapped player to score a century in IPL playoffs (2022). IPL 2025 Champion as captain of RCB. Leading run-scorer in Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy 2024–25 (428 runs). Fastest century in Ranji Trophy history (68 balls). Rajat Patidar has evolved from a fringe domestic player to a key figure in Indian cricket, both as a player and a leader.

A.B.DE Villiers


Abraham Benjamin de Villiers (born 17 February 1984) is a South African former international cricketer. He is regarded as one of the greatest batters of his generation.[3] de Villiers was named as the ICC ODI Player of the Year three times during his 15-year international career.[4] He was one of the five Wisden cricketers of the decade at the end of 2019.[5] He began his international career as a wicket-keeper-batter, but he has most often played only as a batter. Regarded as one of the most innovative and destructive batsmen in the modern era, de Villiers is known for a range of unorthodox shots, particularly behind the wicket-keeper.[6] He made his international debut in a Test match against England in 2004 and first played a One Day International (ODI) in early 2005. His debut in Twenty20 International cricket came in 2006. He scored over 8,000 runs in both Test and ODI cricket and is one of the very few batsmen to have a batting average of over fifty in both forms of the game.[7] In limited overs cricket, he is an attacking player.[8] He holds the record for the fastest ODI fifty (16 balls) equalled by Matthew Forde of the West Indies who made 58 off 19 balls against Ireland,[9] fastest ODI century (31 balls), and fastest ODI 150 (62 balls) De Villiers captained South Africa in all three formats, although after a series of injuries, he stepped down from the Test captaincy. In 2017, he stepped down from captaining the national limited-overs games[10] and in May 2018, at the age of 34, he announced his retirement from all forms of international cricket.[11][12][13] In January 2020, however, de Villiers expressed an interest in making an international comeback and play in the 2020 T20 World Cup,[14] although later in the year it was confirmed that he would not do so.[15][16][17] On 19 November 2021, de Villiers announced his retirement from all forms of cricket.[18] In October 2024, de Villiers became the 8th South African cricketer to be inducted into the ICC Hall of Fame.[19][20]












Chris Gayle

Chris Gayle played for Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) from 2011 to 2017, making a significant impact during his tenure. He is widely celebrated as one of the most destructive batsmen in IPL history and was inducted into the RCB Hall of Fame in 2022 alongside AB de Villiers. Key Achievements with RCB: Scored 3,163 runs in 84 innings for RCB, the third-highest in the team’s history. Hit 175 off 66 balls* against Pune Warriors India in IPL 2013, a record for the highest individual score in IPL history at the time. Achieved the fastest century in IPL history (30 balls) in that same innings. Won the Orange Cap in 2011 and 2012 for being the highest run-scorer in the tournament. Won the Most Valuable Player award in 2011 and was named Man of the Tournament. Legacy: His explosive batting revolutionized the T20 format and made him a fan favorite in Bangalore. The highest individual score (175*) and fastest century records in IPL remain under his name. Though he left RCB after the 2017 season to join Punjab Kings, his contributions are immortalized in RCB’s history and the broader narrative of the IPL.
